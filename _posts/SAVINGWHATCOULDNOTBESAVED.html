SAVING WHAT COULD NOT BE SAVED

Written by

Alexander Dong and Shariar Vaez-Ghaemi

INT. LABORATORY OFFICE - DAY

LESLIE RANDALL, Head of Safety at the Harlock Institute of Biological Discovery, a leading center for biowarfare research, glances between the multiple busy monitors that sit on her desk. She furiously scribbles a note in a notebook and rustles through the papers on her desk to find something. Within the hurricane that is her work flow, a ring is heard. Without looking down and missing a beat, she pins the phone between her shoulder and ear and answers it. She continues to type on her keyboard.

LESLIE

Leslie Randall, Head of Security.

Several seconds of silence.

UNKNOWN (V.O.)

Leslie.

Leslie's eyes immediately widen, she clutches the phone closer.

LESLIE

Chester?

       CHESTER (V.O.)

Leslie, oh my god. Leslie.

LESLIE

Chester, why are you calling me? What do you need?

        CHESTER (V.O.)

They got me.

LESLIE

They what? Who are you talking about?

CHESTER

Leslie, you know who I’m talking about.

LESLIE

                                (beat)

No, no, no, no, no, no.

CHESTER

Leslie.

LESLIE

I told you to stay away from those people.

CHESTER

I know, Leslie, I know.

LESLIE

No, you don’t know. You never know anything, Chester. Those are dangerous people.

CHESTER

(yelling)

You don’t think I know that, Leslie?

Leslie puts her face in her hands and sighs.

CHESTER (CONT’D)

I got kidnapped. I’m looking at a table full of knives and guns right now.

Leslie pulls her eyes above her fingers to show tears and fear.

LESLIE

Chester, why are you calling me?

Silence.

LESLIE

Chester.

CHESTER

They want… the codes.

LESLIE

What codes?

CHESTER

Project Redwing.

LESLIE

                                (beat)

I don’t know what you’re talking about.

CHESTER

They need the codes.

LESLIE

Chester, I’m going to hang up.

CHESTER

Leslie, they’re going to kill me. I don’t have time for you to come to terms with…

LESLIE

Do you know what Project Redwing is, Chester?

Chester doesn’t respond.

 

LESLIE (CONT’D)

It’s our chemical-enhanced drone program. Anyone with access to the drones can spread toxins on an entire city within hours.

CHESTER

Leslie…

Leslie

Why don’t you put them on the phone right now and I’ll explain it to them?

Chester

They don’t want their voice on the phone lines.

Leslie

I just want to explain to them…

Chester

What they already know, Leslie! They know how dangerous this is. That’s why they’re holding me hostage.

Leslie

Ughhh…

Chester

If they thought you’d just hand the codes over, they’d walk into your office and take them.

LESLIE

Chester… You know I can’t do this.

CHESTER

No no no stop PLEASE.

Chest screams in pain over the phone.

LESLIE

CHESTER? CHESTER?

CHESTER

(gasping)

You’re going to kill me. FUCK.

LESLIE

CHESTER? ARE YOU ALRIGHT?

CHESTER

Leslie, listen to me.

LESLIE

Chester…

CHESTER

Please, you need to help me.

LESLIE

(stammering)

Chester… I-I can’t. I can’t.

CHESTER

Please..baby, they’re going to kill me. Fuck, I’m gonna die.

Leslie is frozen.

CHESTER

Leslie, I don’t want to die. Please help me.

Leslie is locked in place, not knowing what to do.

CHESTER

I know you moved on the minute you signed those papers.

LESLIE

You don’t have to do this, Chester.

CHESTER

I know you’re happier without me.

LESLIE

You’re making this harder than it has to be.

Leslie begins to cry. Chester listens to her sobs.

CHESTER

I’m making this my lifeline.

LESLIE

You’ve always…

There is a tense moment of silence.

CHESTER

What?

Leslie stares blankly at her computer screens, her eyes glistening with tears.

CHESTER

(chuckling sadly)

You were going to say that I’ve always made things difficult. That I’ve always tested you.

LESLIE

You’re being ridiculous.

CHESTER

But I’m not wrong, am I?

LESLIE

Chester... Now is not the time for you to be upset. We can’t have this discussion right now.

CHESTER

I didn’t say I’m upset.

(sighs)

Matter of fact, I agree with you.

Leslie rolls her eyes and scoffs.

CHESTER (CONT’D)

No, you’re right Leslie. I did always try to make things hard because I was the one who knew that… saving our marriage wasn’t going to be easy.

Leslie sheds a tear as she bites her thumb nervously.

CHESTER (CONT’D)

I made it hard because I love you. And do you want to know why you put up with the difficult things for three years, Leslie?

LESLIE

I can’t do this, Chester.

CHESTER

Because you loved me! And you still love me.

LESLIE

(nasally, tearfully)

I’m sorry, Chester.

There is silence on Chester’s line as Leslie lets out a steady flow of tears.

An electric machine is turned on by one of Chester’s kidnappers.

CHESTER

(gasping for breath)

ARGHHH FUCK.

LESLIE

(horrified)

Chester! Chester! Wait! Stop! Wait!

Chester stops screaming and the electric machine turns off.

LESLIE

Charlie-six, football-twenty-five, umbrella-eighty-seven, sequoia-fifty-four.

CHESTER

Oh, Leslie. Oh, thank you, thank you.

LESLIE

I can't lose you.

CHESTER

I owe you my life, Leslie.

LESLIE

(still crying)

I just… I want you to be safe.

CHESTER

                                (beat)

(softly, quickly)

Thank you. Thank you, thank you, thank you, thank you.

LESLIE

Are they gone?

CHESTER

Thank you. Thank you, thank you, thank you, thank you.

LESLIE

Chester, are they gone?

There is a moment of silence, broken by a short chuckle from Chester.

LESLIE (CONT’D)

Chester, why are you laughing?

CHESTER

(through chuckles)

Remember how… remember how you almost said that I’ve always tested you.

LESLIE

Chester… what the hell is going on?

CHESTER

Well, what can I say, Leslie. You know me like nobody else.

Leslie furrows her brows. She stares at her phone before talking.

LESLIE

Chester… tell me you didn’t just…

CHESTER

Didn’t just what, Leslie? Didn’t just make up my own kidnapping? Didn’t just see how much you’d be willing to sacrifice just to save me?

Leslie breathes heavily and touches her forehead with her hand.

CHESTER (CONT’D)

(aggressively, smiling)

Well I fucking did it, Leslie! I made it up.

LESLIE

You did this all for my attention?

CHESTER

(shouting)

No! Let’s make that clear. I could always get your attention whenever I wanted. You just didn’t think I deserved it.

LESLIE

Then why did you do this?

CHESTER

You still love me. You just never wanted to accept it.

LESLIE

You might be right, Chester. But the only reason I love you is because I’m an idiot!

CHESTER

(chuckling)

Then we’re perfect for each other.

LESLIE

No. No, Chester. Because here’s the thing… being in love with you is miserable.

CHESTER

You don’t mean that.

LESLIE

I mean it. Because you do shit like this, and literally nobody else in the world does.

CHESTER

Leslie-

LESLIE

I’ve dated so many men in my life. None of them would call me in the middle of the day and con me into reading them bioweapon access codes.

Chester

[stuttering] We got divorced four months ago. You’ve been in three relationships since then?

Leslie takes a deep breath.

CHESTER

Well, I’ll give you this, Leslie. How many of them made you feel the way I made you feel while we were together?

LESLIE

You mean how many of them made me feel manic?

CHESTER

I–... no.

LESLIE

None of them made me feel that way, Chester. That’s why I’m happy now.

CHESTER

So, you really were unhappy while we were together?

Several seconds of silence pass as Chester is reeling in disappointment and Leslie almost feels guilty for the pain she has caused.

LESLIE

Chester, we don’t choose who we fall in love with. But… we choose who we spend our time with.

Leslie stares at a picture of herself and Chester on the wall, in which Chester is smiling and Leslie is frowning.

LESLIE

When the person who makes you love also makes you cry, that’s when you try to change your life for the better.

Chester

I called you today to show you something that you knew but didn’t want to admit.

Leslie

And I did that for you instead?

Chester’s tears are audible over the phone.

CHESTER

I’m sorry, Leslie. I’m sorry for everything. For today, for our marriage, for our divorce.

LESLIE

Chester, I just… I want you to move on. Because I did that a long time ago.

Leslie and Chester sit in tense, sorrowful silence.

CHESTER

I will. I will try. Goodbye, Leslie.

LESLIE

Chester...

CHESTER

(quickly, hopefully)

Yes, Leslie?

LESLIE

I’m going to change my phone number.

CHESTER

Yeah.
